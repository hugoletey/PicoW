The Components

  • Raspberry Pi Pico W

    The Pico W board is a powerful and versatile microcontroller based on the RP2040 chip. It offers excellent value for money and comes with many features including:

    • Dual-core ARM Cortex M0+ processor running at 133 MHz
    • 264 KB of RAM
    • Communication interfaces: USB 2.0, SPI, I2C, UART
    • Programmable GPIO pins
    • Low power consumption

  • DHT22 Sensor (AM2302)

    The DHT22 sensor is an economical and accurate digital sensor that measures both temperature and relative humidity. It communicates with the Pico W board via a serial communication interface and offers the following features:

    • Temperature measurement range: -40°C to 80°C
    • Humidity measurement range: 0% to 100%
    • Temperature accuracy: ±0.5°C
    • Humidity accuracy: ±2% RH
    • Low power consumption
    • Simple serial communication interface

  • Jumper Wires

    Jumper wires are flexible connection cables that allow establishing electrical connections between the Pico W board and the DHT22 sensor. They are available in different lengths and colors, and their use facilitates prototyping and circuit wiring.

  • Computer with USB Port

    A computer is required to program the Pico W board and to display temperature and humidity data. The computer must have a USB port to connect the Pico W board and development software compatible with MicroPython.

    Note: It is essential to always flash the memory of the PICO W when handling it in order to upload another code.
